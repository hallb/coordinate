# Vision and Problem Statement

## Problem Statement

Canadian families covered by multiple health and dental plans -- whether through employers, self-employment (incorporated PHSPs), or a combination -- face a fragmented, multi-step claim submission process that is confusing, time-consuming, and leads to missed reimbursements:

- **Multi-plan complexity**: When a family has two or more plans (e.g., both spouses with employer coverage, or one employed and one self-employed with a PHSP), a single expense like a child's dental filling can require 3-5 separate submissions -- to the primary plan, the secondary plan, and then a Health Care Spending Account -- each requiring the EOB from the previous step.
- **Confusing COB rules**: Determining which plan is primary depends on the claimant (employee-first rule) and, for dependents, on parent birthdays (the "birthday rule"). Mistakes cause denials and delays.
- **Siloed insurer tools**: Each insurer's app (Sun Life, Manulife, Blue Cross, etc.) only manages claims for its own plan. No consumer tool orchestrates the end-to-end COB workflow across insurers.
- **HCSA as an afterthought**: HCSAs can cover remaining out-of-pocket costs after insurance pays, but many families forget to submit or don't realize what's eligible, leaving employer-provided dollars unspent.
- **Receipt and document chaos**: Tracking receipts, EOBs, submission deadlines, remaining annual maximums, and per-category limits across multiple dependents and benefit types is a manual, error-prone burden.
- **Unused benefits expire silently**: Annual and per-category maximums reset at plan year boundaries. Families often don't realize they have unused coverage -- massage therapy, vision, paramedical -- until it's too late, effectively losing benefits they've paid premiums for.
- **Money left on the table**: The combined effect is that families routinely under-claim, miss reimbursements they're entitled to, and let paid-for benefits expire unused.

## Vision

Coordinate is a single place where Canadian families manage their complete insurance claim lifecycle -- from initial expense through final HCSA reimbursement -- across every plan, every dependent, and every benefit category. Beyond reactive claim submission, Coordinate proactively surfaces unused coverage and helps families plan the timing of discretionary expenses around plan year boundaries and remaining maximums. No eligible dollar goes unclaimed, and no paid-for benefit expires unused. As a byproduct, the record of what was spent and what remains unreimbursed provides a ready-made input for CRA medical expense tax credit reporting.

## Mission

Encode Canadian coordination of benefits rules into software that guides families through the correct submission order, tracks each claim's status across plans, manages receipts and EOBs, monitors remaining balances and maximums, surfaces clear next steps, and proactively alerts families to expiring coverage and optimal timing for discretionary care -- so that managing insurance claims requires minimal effort and zero expertise.

## Value Proposition

For Canadian families juggling multiple health plans -- employer groups, self-employed PHSPs, and spending accounts -- Coordinate eliminates the cognitive overhead of multi-plan claims management. It replaces spreadsheets, shoe-boxes of receipts, and guesswork with an automated workflow that maximizes reimbursement, prevents missed deadlines, and provides a single source of truth for all insurance-related expenses and documents.
